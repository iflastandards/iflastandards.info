Preliminary assessment

Carry out a preliminary assessment of the manifestation before recording a description.

Record a separate description for a distinct manifestation.

Consider a manifestation to be distinct from other manifestations if there is a significant difference in any of the following characteristics:

  • category of carrier
  • name of publisher or producer
  • place of publication or production
  • date of publication or production

Consider a manifestation that is produced to be exemplified by one and only one item.

The preliminary assessment determines the granularity and completeness of the description. The granularity of a manifestation is dependent on the presence of part manifestations, known as units, and the number of expressions that comprise the manifestation. The completeness of a manifestation is dependent on the intention to publish or produce it in a single contiguous process or in a set of discrete processes over time.

The assessment covers three distinct characteristics that apply to every manifestation:

  • A manifestation may be published or produced in one or more units.
  • A manifestation may embody one or more expressions that realise one or more works.
  • A manifestation may embody the content of a work that is planned to be static or to change over time.

Each characteristic has a binary either-or categorisation at the broadest level. One of the two categories for each characteristic must apply to every manifestation:

  • A manifestation is in either one unit or more than one unit.
  • A manifestation embodies either one expression or more than one expression.
  • A manifestation embodies expressions of works that are either static or are planned to be embodied over time.

These characteristics are mutually independent and are analysed in separate stages. The order of analyses is not important; ISBDM recommends following the order given here.
